Better to be a warrior in a garden, than a gardener in a war.

The saying "Better to be a warrior in a garden than a gardener in a war" emphasizes the value of being prepared for adversity, even in times of peace. A warrior in a garden symbolizes someone who possesses strength, discipline, and combat skills but chooses to live peacefully, ready to defend if necessary. In contrast, a gardener in a war represents someone unprepared for conflict, left vulnerable when faced with danger. This proverb underscores the importance of cultivating resilience and preparedness, ensuring that one can handle life's challenges rather than being caught defenseless when hardship arises.
